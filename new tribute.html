Biography

Lawrence Edward Page born March 26, 1973 is an American computer scientist and Internet entrepreneur who co-founded Google.

Page is the CEO of Google's parent company, Alphabet Inc. one of the biggest IT companies in the world. After stepping aside as Google CEO in August 2001 in favor of Eric Schmidt, he re-assumed the role in April 2011. He announced his intention to step aside a second time in July 2015 to become CEO of Alphabet, under which Google's assets would be reorganized. Under Page, Alphabet is seeking to deliver major advancements in a variety of industries. Page is the inventor of PageRank, Google's best-known search ranking algorithm, Trough Page’s leadership Google became one of the top companies in the world, best known for their search engine, Android operating system which is the most used mobile operating system in the world, and the G suite of apps which includes Gmail, Drive, Docs and more.

It’s the aspiration for anyone with a passion for IT and coding to get hired by Google, they are the most inspiring and influential company today in the internet era that we live in. In the process of building Google to what it is today Page became one of the richest people alive, with an estimated net worth of US$40.7 billion.

My job as a leader is to make sure everybody in the company has great opportunities, and that they feel they're having a meaningful impact and are contributing to the good of society. As a world, we're doing a better job of that. My goal is for Google to lead, not follow that.

Larry Page, CEO, Alphabet Inc.
